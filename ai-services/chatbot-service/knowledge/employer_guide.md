# Employer Guide — Hiring on SBOUP

## Step 1 — Create your employer account

1. Open the SBOUP app and tap **Create Account**.
2. Enter your name, email, and phone number.
3. Select your role: **Employer**.
4. Add your organisation name and location.
5. Verify your email address.

## Step 2 — Post an opportunity

Go to your **Dashboard** and tap **Post Opportunity**.

### What to include in a good posting

| Field | Tips |
|---|---|
| Title | Be specific — "React Frontend Developer" beats "Developer Needed" |
| Description | Describe the work, responsibilities, and what success looks like |
| Required skills | Add every skill you need — this drives candidate matching |
| Category | Formal, Contract, Freelance, or Apprenticeship |
| Location | City/district, or mark as Remote |
| Experience level | Entry (0–2 yrs), Mid (2–5 yrs), Senior (5+ yrs), or Any |
| Compensation range | Min and max in UGX — workers filter by this |
| Application deadline | Sets urgency and filters active candidates |

### Why required skills matter

The AI matches candidates based on skill overlap. If you list 5 required
skills, a candidate with all 5 scores much higher than one with 2. Be
specific — "Node.js" scores better than "Backend".

## Step 3 — Review applications

Once your posting is live, applications arrive ranked by match score.

From your **Dashboard**:
- Tap your posting to see all applicants.
- Applicants are sorted by match score (highest first).
- Tap any applicant to view their full profile, skills, and experience.
- Download their generated CV directly from the application.

## Step 4 — Shortlist candidates

When you find a strong candidate:
1. Tap **Shortlist** on their application.
2. The candidate is notified and moved to your shortlist.
3. You can message them directly through the Messaging Hub.

## Step 5 — Extend an offer

When you are ready to hire:
1. Open the candidate's application.
2. Tap **Extend Offer**.
3. The candidate receives a notification and can accept or decline.

## Step 6 — Manage your postings

From your Dashboard you can:
- **Edit** a posting to update details or extend the deadline.
- **Pause** a posting to stop receiving new applications temporarily.
- **Close** a posting once you have hired.
- **Repost** a closed posting to hire again for the same role.

## Tips for better results

- **Complete your employer profile** — candidates trust verified employers.
- **Respond quickly** — candidates who are shortlisted promptly are more
  likely to stay engaged.
- **Be specific with skills** — vague skill lists produce lower-quality matches.
- **Set a realistic compensation range** — postings with no salary range
  receive fewer applications.
- **Use the Remote flag** — remote postings attract candidates from all
  locations, not just your city.
