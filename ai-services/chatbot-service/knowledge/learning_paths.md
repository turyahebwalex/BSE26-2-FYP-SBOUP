# Learning Paths and Upskilling on SBOUP

## What is the Upskill section?

The Upskill section identifies the skills you are missing for jobs you want,
and recommends free or affordable learning resources to close those gaps.
When you complete a course and add the skill to your profile, your match
scores for relevant jobs improve immediately.

## How to access learning paths

1. Go to the **Upskill** tab from the bottom navigation.
2. You will see a list of your skill gaps — skills that appear frequently
   in jobs you have viewed or applied for, but are not on your profile.
3. Each skill gap shows:
   - How many jobs require it
   - Your current match score impact
   - Recommended learning resources

## Starting a learning path

1. Tap the **Bridge** button next to a skill gap.
2. A personalised learning path opens with:
   - Free resources (YouTube, Coursera free tier, government training)
   - Estimated time to complete
   - Milestones to track your progress
3. Work through the resources at your own pace.
4. Mark milestones as complete to track progress.

## Adding a skill after completing a course

Once you have completed a learning path:
1. Go to your **Profile** → **Edit Skills**.
2. Add the new skill with the appropriate proficiency level.
3. Your match scores update automatically.

Start at **Beginner** or **Intermediate** — do not claim Expert unless you
genuinely are. Employers verify skills during interviews.

## Recommended learning resources by skill category

### Technical skills
- **freeCodeCamp** (freecodecamp.org) — web development, Python, data
- **The Odin Project** (theodinproject.com) — full-stack web development
- **Kaggle** (kaggle.com) — data science and machine learning
- **CS50** (cs50.harvard.edu) — free Harvard computer science course

### Creative skills
- **Canva Design School** (canva.com/learn) — graphic design basics
- **YouTube** — search for "Figma tutorial", "Lightroom tutorial", etc.
- **Coursera** (coursera.org) — UI/UX design, photography

### Trade skills
- **UVQF** — Uganda Vocational Qualifications Framework courses
- **YouTube** — practical tutorials for carpentry, plumbing, electrical
- **Local polytechnics** — hands-on training in Kampala and major towns

### Business skills
- **Google Digital Skills for Africa** — free digital marketing, analytics
- **Coursera** — accounting, project management (audit for free)
- **LinkedIn Learning** — business and management skills

## How skill gaps are identified

The AI looks at:
1. Jobs you have viewed or applied for
2. Jobs with high match scores that you are close to qualifying for
3. Skills that appear most frequently in your target job category

It then shows you the gaps that, if closed, would have the biggest impact
on your match scores.

## How long does upskilling take?

It depends on the skill:
- Basic digital skills (Excel, Canva) — 1–2 weeks
- Intermediate technical skills (React, Python basics) — 4–8 weeks
- Trade skills (Carpentry, Electrical) — 3–6 months with practice
- Advanced skills (Machine Learning, Accounting) — 3–12 months

The learning path shows an estimated time for each resource.

## Does completing a course automatically update my profile?

No — you need to manually add the skill to your profile after completing
the course. The platform does not automatically verify external courses.
This is intentional: you should only add skills you are genuinely ready
to use in a job.
