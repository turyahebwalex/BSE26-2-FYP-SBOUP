# How Match Scores Work on SBOUP

## What is a match score?

Your match score (0–100%) shows how well your profile fits a specific job
opportunity. It is calculated automatically by the AI every time you view
a job. The higher the score, the better the fit.

A score of 80%+ means you are a strong candidate.
A score of 50–79% means you are a reasonable fit with some gaps.
A score below 50% means there are significant mismatches.

## What affects your score

### 1. Skill overlap (most important signal)

The AI compares your skills against the job's required skills.

- **Exact matches** score highest — if the job needs "Python" and you have
  "Python", that is a direct hit.
- **Proficiency level matters** — having "Python" at Expert level scores
  higher than having it at Beginner level.
- **Skill category overlap** — if the job is in the Technical category and
  you have Technical skills (even if not the exact same ones), you score
  higher than a worker with only Trade skills.

### 2. Skill gap

The number of required skills you are missing lowers your score. If a job
needs 5 skills and you have 3 of them, you have a gap of 2. Closing that
gap (by adding skills or completing learning paths) raises your score.

### 3. Location match

If you are in the same city as the job, or the job is marked as Remote,
you get a location match bonus. If you are in a different city and the job
is not remote, your score is lower.

### 4. Salary fit

If the job's compensation range overlaps with your expected rate range,
you get a salary fit bonus. If there is no overlap (e.g. you expect
UGX 2,000,000/month but the job pays UGX 500,000–800,000), your score
is lower.

### 5. Experience level fit

Jobs specify an experience level requirement:
- **Entry** — 0–2 years (0–24 months)
- **Mid** — 2–5 years (24–60 months)
- **Senior** — 5+ years (60+ months)
- **Any** — no requirement

If your total months of experience meet the requirement, you get an
experience fit bonus.

### 6. Profile completeness

A more complete profile scores slightly higher across all jobs. This
rewards workers who have invested in their profile.

## Score ranges explained

| Score | What it means |
|---|---|
| 85–100% | Excellent match — you meet almost all requirements |
| 65–84% | Strong match — minor gaps, still a great candidate |
| 45–64% | Moderate match — some gaps but worth applying |
| 25–44% | Weak match — significant skill or experience gaps |
| 0–24% | Poor match — this job is not suited to your current profile |

## How to improve your score for a specific job

1. **Open the job details** and tap "View Match Breakdown" to see exactly
   which skills you are missing.
2. **Add missing skills** to your profile if you genuinely have them.
3. **Complete a learning path** for skills you do not have yet — once you
   finish, add the skill to your profile.
4. **Update your location** if you have moved.
5. **Adjust your expected rate** if it is outside the job's compensation range.
6. **Complete your profile** — add bio, experience, and portfolio items.

## Why does a completely different job score low?

This is correct behaviour. A carpenter should score near 0% for a software
developer role, and vice versa. The AI is working as intended — it is telling
you that job is not a match for your skills. Focus on jobs that score 45%+.

## Why do multiple jobs score the same?

If several jobs all score the same (e.g. 19%), it usually means you have
zero skill overlap with all of them. The small baseline score comes from
profile completeness and experience. The fix is to add more skills to your
profile that match the types of jobs you want.
