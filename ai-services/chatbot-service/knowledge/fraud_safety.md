# Fraud Prevention and Safety on SBOUP

## How SBOUP protects you

SBOUP uses an AI fraud detection system that automatically screens every
job posting before it goes live. The system flags and removes postings that
show signs of fraud, scams, or unsafe content.

In addition, the community reporting system allows users to flag suspicious
postings, which are reviewed and removed quickly.

## Signs of a fraudulent job posting

Be cautious of any posting that:

- **Asks for money** — legitimate employers never ask workers to pay a
  registration fee, training fee, or deposit to get a job.
- **Asks for personal banking details** — never share your bank account
  number, mobile money PIN, or national ID number with an employer through
  the platform.
- **Offers unrealistically high pay** — if a job offers UGX 5,000,000/month
  for basic work with no experience required, it is likely a scam.
- **Has vague or no job description** — legitimate postings describe the
  actual work clearly.
- **Asks you to communicate outside the platform immediately** — scammers
  often try to move conversations to WhatsApp or email to avoid detection.
- **Pressures you to decide immediately** — legitimate employers give you
  time to consider an offer.
- **Has poor spelling and grammar** — while not always a sign of fraud,
  very poorly written postings from "international companies" are a red flag.

## What to do if you see a suspicious posting

1. Open the job posting.
2. Tap the **Report** button (flag icon).
3. Select the reason:
   - Fake or fraudulent posting
   - Inappropriate content
   - Misleading information
   - Asking for payment
   - Other
4. Add any additional details and tap **Submit**.

Our team reviews all reports within 24 hours. Confirmed fraudulent postings
are removed and the employer account is suspended.

## What to do if you have been scammed

If you have already been scammed through a posting on SBOUP:

1. Report the posting immediately using the steps above.
2. Do not send any more money or personal information.
3. Contact your bank or mobile money provider to reverse any transactions
   if possible.
4. Report to the Uganda Police Cybercrime Unit if money was lost.
5. Contact SBOUP support through the Help section for further assistance.

## Protecting your account

- **Use a strong password** — at least 8 characters with numbers and symbols.
- **Do not share your login details** with anyone, including people claiming
  to be SBOUP staff. SBOUP staff will never ask for your password.
- **Log out on shared devices** — always log out if you use a shared phone
  or computer.
- **Enable notifications** — so you are alerted to any activity on your account.

## For employers — keeping your postings safe

- Only post genuine opportunities. Fraudulent postings result in permanent
  account suspension.
- Do not ask candidates for money at any stage of the hiring process.
- Communicate through the platform's Messaging Hub — it keeps a record and
  protects both parties.
- Verify candidate identities before making offers for sensitive roles.

## SBOUP's commitment

SBOUP does not tolerate fraud, scams, or exploitation on the platform.
Every posting is screened by AI before going live. Community reports are
reviewed within 24 hours. Accounts found to be fraudulent are permanently
banned.

If you have concerns about platform safety, contact support through the
**Help** section in the app.
